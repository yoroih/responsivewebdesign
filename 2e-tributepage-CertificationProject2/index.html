Aaron Swartz

"Be curious. Read widely. Try new things. I think a lor of what people call intelligence boils down to curiosity."

Here's a time line of Aaron Swartz life:

  • 1986: Birth of Aaron Swartz on November 8th in Chicago, Illinois.
  • 1999: At the age of 13, he develops the website "The Info Network," which later becomes Infogami.
  • 2004: Contributes to the development of the RSS 1.0 format and co-authors the RDF Site Summary specifications.
  • 2005: Takes part in the founding of Reddit, a social platform for web bookmarks.
  • 2008: Launches the Open Library project, an initiative to create a publicly accessible digital library.
  • 2010: Downloads and shares a large number of academic articles from JSTOR through MIT's network, leading to his subsequent arrest.
  • 2011: Co-founds Demand Progress, an online organization dedicated to promoting freedom of expression and privacy on the Internet.
  • 2011: Becomes a prominent figure in the fight against the Stop Online Piracy Act (SOPA) and the Protect Intellectual Property Act (PIPA).
  • 2013: Tragically, Aaron Swartz takes his own life on January 11th at the age of 26.

    • After his death:

  • Many organizations and Internet freedom advocates continue to work online in line with the ideals Swartz defended.
  • The documentary "The Internet's Own Boy: The Story of Aaron Swartz" is released in 2014, highlighting his life and legacy.
  • Scholarships and programs are established in memory of Aaron Swartz to support open access and online justice.
